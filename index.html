Inadequate water supply and sanitation remains an intractable challenge with dire consequences on the environment, livelihoods, and public health. Poor and marginalized groups, particularly women, girls and ethnic minorities are disproportionately affected by these consequences. The challenges of water and sanitation are not only local but are also transnational. This requires an expansion and revitalization of international co-operation, and capacity building to support water & sanitation related activities and programs.
The All4WasH project brings together the NTNU, three African Centers of Excellence in water supply and sanitation in Ethiopia, Ghana, and Tanzania , and their alliance Universities in Mali (National School of Engineers Bamako), Uganda (Kampala International University) and South Africa (University of Venda) to confront some of the local and global challenges of water supply and sanitation through mutual co-operation.

· 2. Strengthen the internalization of sustainable water supply& sanitation education and research in Norway and the partner Universities.·

3. Improve gender equity and inclusion of marginalized groups in education and research.mic staff exchange, joint research projects, and co-supervision.

Objective

1. develop relevant curriculum incorporating eLearning & blended learning to equip students with the transversal skills needed to be active participants in the water and sanitation sectors considering the SDGs.
2. establish research hubs to increase the quality of research and advance knowledge in the areas of circular economy, digitalization, governance, equity & inclusion, resource recovery, health, and climate change.
3. further provide a platform for Norwegian and partner Universities students to work on joint research projects to cross-fertilize ideas, innovate and develop solutions from different cultural perspectives
4. establish long term partnerships at the institutional and individual levels to continue to pursue these activities of mutual interest beyond the lifespan of the project.
